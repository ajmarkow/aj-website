---
title: "On Building Things That Look Like They Were Made"
date: 2026-05-28
description: "On the overlap between design craft and software craft — and the tells that reveal whether someone was paying attention."
---

The thing about studying design before engineering is that you learn to care about the evidence of effort — not the effort itself, but what the finished thing shows of it. A letterpress print tells you the impression was made under pressure. A contact sheet tells you the photographer worked the scene. The detail is how you know someone was paying attention.

Good software has the same tells. Not necessarily in the UI — though that matters — but in the architecture. In where the boundaries are drawn, in what has been extracted from what, in the names things were given. Sloppy code and sloppy design share a failure mode: they both hide the thinking. The work that holds up is the work that reveals the care taken to make it look effortless.
